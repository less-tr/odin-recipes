SHRIMP ADOBO

A savory and tangy Filipino dish made with tender shrimp simmered in adobo sauce — a rich blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.

SHRIMP ADOBO RECIPE

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g shrimp (with shell on or peeled, depending on preference)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced or crushed
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup water (optional, to adjust sauce consistency)
  • 1 small onion, sliced (optional)
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste>
  • Green onions or chili (optional, for garnish or added spice)>

PREPARATIONS

  1. Marinate the shrimp:
    2.
      In a bowl, combine the shrimp with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Let it marinate for 15-30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  2. Sauté aromatics (optional):
    3.
      Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onions until softened, then add the garlic from the marinade.
  3. Cook the shrimp:
    4.
      Add the marinated shrimp and the rest of the marinade to the pan. Simmer over medium heat until the shrimp turns pink and is cooked through (5-7min).
      If the sauce reduces too quickly, add a bit of water.
  4. Adjust seasoning:
    5.
      Taste the sauce and season with additional salt or black pepper as needed. If you prefer it less tangy, reduce the vinegar slightly or add a pinch of sugar.
  5. Serve:
    6.
      Serve hot with steamed rice. Garnish with chopped green onions or chili for a spicy kick.
